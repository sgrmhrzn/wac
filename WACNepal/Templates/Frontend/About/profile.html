Introduction to WAC-Nepal:

WAC- Nepal(Name changed by the 5th General Assembly of Oppressed Class and Women Awareness Center OCWAC-Achham ) is a locally emerged NGO registered under the Achham District Administration Office (Registration No 061/054) and is allied to Social Welfare Council (Affiliation No 7057/055) and affiliated with NGO Federation Nepal (Affiliation No. M 04/054). It is affiliation with Human Right and Social Justice Forum – Nepal and NANGAN-Nepal. The main objective of this organization is to work towards increasing the level of low-income groups and economically and socially backward populations. WAC-Nepal has total number of96General Members and 126 full time Staff. Its executive member comprises of nine, out of whom three are Female and six are Male.

Working districts of WAC- Nepal :

WAC has been working with rural communities of districts of Far-Western Development Region, Nepal. It has made plan to go over far and Mid-Western districts of the country within five years. The Human Development Index of Achham District is 0.325 and it ranks 69 in 75 districts of Nepal; Gender Development Index (GDI) of Achham is 0.275. All other remaining districts of the region is similar in HDI and GDI index. All are hill districts where geography is rough and access is difficult. Social discrimination and low economic status is the common issues of them. Agriculture based economy of the districts have entrenched problems such as monsoon fed irrigation, lack of agricultural equipments and materials, inaccessibility of market and lack of basic infrastructure. The family size of the districts is more than five in comparison to their earnings. The majority of the population in working districts areBhramin and Chhetri including ethnic and minority groups. Female literacy rate and other developmental indexes are lower than male. The disparity between upper class and lower class is high as national figure. Unlike national average, rural population is more than 90% in these districts, which is also one major indicators of underdevelopment. Overall literacy rate of the districts is less than 45percent. Children have to walk in mountainous and dangerous slopes to reach schools, which are overcrowded, with insufficient teachers and other basic student facilities. Many schools and health posts lack proper infrastructure ; the clear drinking water facilities and road head access are the far away to reach the targeted community, donkey, sheepetc are the main means of transportation to deliver the goods and equipments to many district of the regions.

The mortality rate of the area is below the rate of national level. The health posts are lacking the health worker. Junior levels of staff are chandellingseniors in some of the cases, ambulances are not easily accessible for the people. They have to use DOKOas stretcher (DOKO as a means of transporting patients to the nearest health clinics.) Medicines are not sufficient virtually no exist in the clinics. Due to low income, people cannot afford expensive medicines. So, the people are at the hand of nature for their life. The health service centers are not sufficient with the comparison of size of the population. Only one hospital is serving to entire population of district. Health posts and sub health posts have neither sufficient equipments nor medicines to serve local people.

Migration for livelihood, poverty, malnutrition, ill-health condition, food deficit and social discrimination are the common issues of the all far-west and mid-west region. In order to play an active role for forming sustainable development and community spiraling processes, WAC-Nepal will apply following strategies to achieve the following vision, mission, and goal.

Vision:

"Formation of fulfilled society is the interest/ needs of WAC-Nepal"

Mission:

To brings fulfillments in target groups through social transformation.

Goal:

To bring improvement in the stages and the area of capacity building of target groups by improving sustainable livelihood, health and long- lives, disaster management, good governance and security or organizational development.

Objectives:

To develop subject-specific manpower for the organizational development.
  1. To conduct training, seminar and awareness programs for empowerment and capacity buildings on targeted communities.
  2. To conduct programs for advocate on the issues of health and right for the old people, female and children through formal and informal education.
  3. To conduct income oriented program to bring improvement on the economic status of target communities.
  4. To advocate for decreasing the present social, gender, educational, caste and religious discrimination in target community.
  5. To implement various programs by contacting, agreement, and coordinating with governmental and non- governmental organizations.
  6. To implement alternative electricity, plantation, and awareness programs for the environment conservation and health improvement.
  7. To conduct various programs for small industry based on agriculture and jungle.
  8. To conduct awareness programs for problems which are seeing in district likes H.I.V and AIDS, reproductive health, drinking water, and cleanness and emergency infestation.
  9. To distribute rescue and helping materials and pre-disaster readiness.
  10. To advocate cooperatively for social issues of community.
  11. To doself improvement of the organization for making specific quality of social service.
  12. Improving the livelihood of beneficiaries for quality life by promoting access to local potential resources.
  13. Strengthening the effectiveness and efficiency of organizational capacity.

Core Value:

A = Accountability.
We keep ourselves accountable to our stakeholders for being consistent with these principles, and ask others to help us do so, not only in our programming, but in all that we do.

C = Commitment to Service
We see the feelings of commitment to services in all the representative, general members, staffs and volunteers of WAC-Nepal, as a result the community believes on WAC.

E =Excellence.
We constantly challenge ourselves to the highest levels of learning and performance to achieve greater impact.

I = Innovative and Creativity
WAC-Nepal innovates new things, perspectives and principles which are new and acceptable for that area and also can be seen in the behavior of all of the manpower. We endeavor to do small thing in different ways with maximum use of local available resources in cost of effective way. We follow innovative and creative way to achieve the organizational goal.

N = Neutrality
Neutrality is another core value of WAC-Nepal. There can be seen a neutrality in all of the representatives,members, staffs, and volunteers in every context either in organizational or in out of organizational programs. Our services and advocates don’t take side to anyone but tries to make feelin win and win situation to the beneficiaries.

R= Right based Advocacy
WAC advocates to ensure the rights provided by government for all and considers the rights-based approach (RBA) as an approach in the fight against poverty and hunger. Empowerment of people to claim their rights by fighting the causes of poverty and hunger is the key of RBA.

T =Team work.
The principle, "The main handle of social development is Team Work", is adopted by WAC-Nepal and we follow it to do any task. We do contact and coordination to our internal and external groups for team work and we find this principle in all of our representatives, members, and staffs.Our service and advocacy is the product of team work. Opportunities and challenges are shareable to all.
